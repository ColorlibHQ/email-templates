The essay
The unbundling of the design tool
By Maya Chen · 7 min read
For a decade, the design tool was a monolith. Figma sat at the center: vector editor, prototyping, comments, design systems, dev handoff, all in one canvas. The argument was always the same — collaboration only works when the surface is shared. And it worked. Until the AI moment shifted what "the surface" even means.
What's happening now is not a competitor. It's an unbundling. The "draw a rectangle, add a component" parts are being subsumed into adjacent products: Cursor for UI scaffolds, Granola for meeting-to-mockup, Linear for spec-to-flow. None of them are design tools. All of them are reducing the surface area where a design tool was load-bearing.
The interesting question isn't whether Figma loses. It's what the new shape of the design role is when half the artifacts you used to produce — the wireframe, the spec doc, the handoff file — get generated as a byproduct of someone else's workflow.
